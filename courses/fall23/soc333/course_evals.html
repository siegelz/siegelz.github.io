What advice would you give to another student considering taking this course?
Goran is incredibly kind, knowledgeable and highly invested in this class. The reading load is very manageable and so are the assignments. Must take if you're interested in any of the topics covered.
I strongly recommend having two or more questions/comments ready after completing the readings for the week so that you have something ready to discuss/propose in class. This will make engagement a bit easier and class more interesting!
Take this class! I loved this class and Professor Blix is incredibly supportive of students. I was able to learn from the other students in the class and reflect on my own education. I would recommend this course to anyone, even if it's not necessary for any requirement.
This course is a must take if you (1) want to investigate the role of education in democratic societies and/or (2) like any of the following: political theory, philosophy, critical theory, history, film, literature, and creative non-fiction. This is comfortably among my favorite courses I've taken here (even though it was over Zoom!). The syllabus is really ambitious and covers a really broad intersection of people, times, places, and topics, but professor Blix was very proactive at making necessary adjustments as we went along the semester. The assignments allow for a lot of creativity and are a really welcome departure from standards in-class essays. (Also, a shoutout to professor Blix for being my only professor this semester to bring up dean Dolan's email about reducing workloads. His adjustments to our final assignments were beyond helpful, and I'm very thankful.)
Take it. Hands down.
This class, and classes like it, is the reason you came to college. Professor Blix is a one of a kind teacher whose passion and interest in the subject matter is matched only by how much he cares for his students. In what was undoubtably the most difficult semester of his teaching career and our learning experience, professor Blix produced a masterpiece of a course that was the highlight of the semester for me. The subject matter was extremely interesting and relevant, after all we are all in the midst of a very intense educational experience and analyzing/evaluating it is important. What makes this class stand out to me is the fact that we were all part of the learning/teaching process. I learned so much from my classmates as well as professor Blix who masterfully guided the discussion while allowing us to debate difficult topics. It is the best course I have taken so far at Princeton and I wish everyone were so lucky as to have a professor as talented and understanding as professor Blix.
