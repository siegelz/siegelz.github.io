Personal Description

I am a senior in the Department of Computer Science at Princeton University. I am interested in building and evaluating AI systems that can reason, plan, and act in complex environments. One focus is on combining approaches from natural language processing and bayesian learning to build neuro-symbolic systems that can explicitly plan and make rational decisions. I am also interested in highlighting the limitations of existing evaluation paradigms for AI agents and systems.

I also study philosophy, where I have interests in decision theory and philosophy of mind.
